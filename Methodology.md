# Methodology

## Objective

The objective of this assignment was to identify and prioritize manufacturing companies that closely match DeepThought's **Federer Profile** by applying the qualification framework (E1, E2 and C3–C8). The goal was to create a structured list of companies that could potentially benefit from AI-driven manufacturing and operational intelligence solutions.

---

# City Selection

After reviewing different manufacturing hubs in India, I selected **Hyderabad** because it has a strong ecosystem of:

* Aerospace & Defence Manufacturing
* Precision Engineering
* Industrial Electronics
* Medical Devices
* Pharmaceutical Manufacturing

These industries have a high concentration of product-based companies with established manufacturing operations.

---

# Research Sources

The research was conducted using publicly available sources, including:

* Official Company Websites
* LinkedIn Company Pages
* Company Careers Pages
* Annual Reports (where available)
* Business Directories (Tofler, Tracxn, Startup India where applicable)
* Public News Articles and Press Releases

---

# Research Process

### Step 1 – Building the Initial List

I first created a broad list of manufacturing companies operating in Hyderabad by using company websites, LinkedIn, business directories, and public search results.

---

### Step 2 – Qualification (E1 & E2)

Each company was checked against the mandatory qualification criteria.

**E1 – Producer**

I verified whether the company manufactures physical products instead of only providing services or acting as a distributor.

**E2 – Accessibility**

I confirmed that the company has active manufacturing operations and enough publicly available information to evaluate its business.

Companies that failed either of these criteria were excluded from further consideration.

---

### Step 3 – Federer Scoring

Companies that passed E1 and E2 were evaluated using the Federer framework.

The following criteria were considered:

* **C3:** Product Differentiation
* **C4:** Decision Maker Quality
* **C5:** Industry Growth Potential
* **C6:** Business Growth Signals
* **C7:** Systems & Operational Maturity
* **C8:** Leadership Depth

Scores were assigned based on publicly available information such as manufacturing capabilities, leadership profiles, certifications, hiring activity, expansion announcements, and business updates.

---

# Final Selection

The final spreadsheet contains shortlisted companies with:

* Company details
* Qualification status (E1 & E2)
* Federer scores
* Overall verdict
* Personalization hook
* Research sources

The final list focuses on companies that best match the assignment's target profile.

---

# Challenges Faced

Some privately held companies had limited financial and operational information available publicly. In such cases, multiple sources were cross-checked before assigning scores.

Another challenge was distinguishing manufacturing companies from service-oriented businesses, which required careful review of company websites and product portfolios.

---

# Key Learnings

This assignment helped me understand that identifying target companies is not simply about collecting company names. A structured qualification process and evidence-based evaluation are essential to identify businesses that are a strong strategic fit for enterprise AI solutions.

The exercise also improved my skills in company research, public data analysis, business qualification, and organizing findings into a structured dataset.
